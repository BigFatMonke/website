Our Story

Why We
Started

It started on the tennis court. As a competitive player, I noticed something that should have been obvious: nobody was wearing sunscreen. We'd spend hours in direct sun — serving, rallying, grinding through matches — completely exposed. No SPF. No shade. No second thought.

When I started learning about skin cancer, I was stunned. Melanoma is the deadliest form, and UV exposure is the number one cause. Athletes — the very people who spend the most time outdoors — are among the least protected. Not because they don't care. Because nobody ever told them they needed to.

TheUVProject exists to change that. We are a youth-led movement building local chapters to put sun safety tools directly into the hands of athletes.

"I used to think sunscreen was for beach days. Then I realized I was training 15 hours a week in direct sunlight — with zero protection. That's when everything clicked."

— TheUVProject Founder

Our Model

Student-Led Sun Safety
in Sports

We work through school-based chapters that implement simple, repeatable systems to increase sunscreen use among athletes. Each chapter focuses on three core actions.

01 — Access

Provide Sunscreen

Chapters organize sunscreen access for teams — coordinating donations, stocking supplies, and ensuring product is physically available at practice and events.

02 — Routine

Build the Habit

A 30-second pre-practice sun safety routine is introduced and repeated consistently. The goal is behavior change, not a one-time awareness push.

03 — Measurement

Track Usage

Chapters collect simple usage data over time to understand what's working and build a case for expanding the program to more sports programs.

No lectures. No one-time events. Real behavior change — built into the team routine.

What Makes This Different

Most sun safety efforts are one-time events — a table at a fair, a flyer in a locker room. We focus on sustained behavior change within existing team structures.

Works within existing team schedules and practice routines
Student-led, which means peers driving peer behavior
Measurable outcomes, not just awareness
Scalable across sports, seasons, and schools
