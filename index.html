Our Story

Why We
Started

It started on the tennis court. As a competitive player, I noticed something that should have been obvious: nobody was wearing sunscreen. We'd spend hours in direct sun — serving, rallying, grinding through matches — completely exposed. No SPF. No shade. No second thought.

When I started learning about skin cancer, I was stunned. Melanoma is the deadliest form, and UV exposure is the number one cause. Athletes — the very people who spend the most time outdoors — are among the least protected. Not because they don't care. Because nobody ever told them they needed to.

TheUVProject exists to change that. We are a youth-led movement building local chapters to put sun safety tools directly into the hands of athletes.

"I used to think sunscreen was for beach days. Then I realized I was training 15 hours a week in direct sunlight — with zero protection. That's when everything clicked."

— TheUVProject Founder

The Hard Truth

Skin Cancer Is
Serious. Don't Ignore It.

This isn't about getting a bad sunburn. Unprotected UV exposure builds up over years — and the damage is permanent. For athletes who train outdoors daily, the risk compounds fast. Here's what the data actually says:

97,000+

New melanoma cases per year

Melanoma is the most dangerous form of skin cancer. Caught late, it spreads rapidly to lymph nodes and organs — and survival rates drop sharply.

10×

Higher risk for outdoor athletes

Studies show competitive outdoor athletes accumulate UV damage at rates far exceeding the general population — starting as early as high school.

SPF 30

Blocks 97% of UV rays

A single daily application of SPF 30 sunscreen can reduce your risk dramatically. It costs almost nothing. The cost of not using it can be your life.

Our Model

Student-Led Action

We are building a national network of high school and college chapters. Here is how our student leaders drive local change.

Sun-Safe Packing Meet-Ups

Chapters gather to assemble "Sun-Ready Kits" (SPF, lip balm, UV stickers, and information sheets) and personally distribute them at events like local tennis matches, swim meets, and track meets.

Fast Event Activations

No boring lectures. Chapters set up high-energy pop-up info tables at athletic meets, running quick trivia challenges and handing out Sun-Ready Kits that protect our athletes from the sun.

Campus Advocacy

Many schools actually ban students from carrying sunscreen. Our chapters lobby school administrators to change these rules and normalize carrying sunscreen.
